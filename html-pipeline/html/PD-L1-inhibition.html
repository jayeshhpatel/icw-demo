Overview

Target Overview

PD-L1 is a cell-surface protein that binds to the receptors PD-1 and CD80 on activated T cells, B cells, and other myeloid cells.
PD-L1 binding to PD-1 on activated T cells has been found to interfere with T-cell proliferation and to inhibit immune responses.1
Overexpression of PD-L1 on cancer cells and solid tumours may allow these cells to avoid immune detection and elimination.2

Compound Overview

Durvalumab is an anti-PD-L1 mAb, designed to bind PD-L1 and reduce the interaction between PD-L1 and its receptors.
Durvalumab is being clinically evaluated for its potential effects on NSCLC, HNSCC, UC, SCLC, solid tumours, and various other cancers.
